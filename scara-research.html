SCARA Robot Arm Development (Existing Arm Upgrade)

Ongoing research focused on improving an existing SCARA robot arm by designing and integrating cycloidal gearboxes for two joints and developing a robust control system using a Teensy 4.1 microcontroller. Current work includes gearbox design, fabrication, assembly, and early-stage control architecture planning.

SCARA Robot Cycloidal Gearbox 2 Joint Upgrade Teensy 4.1 Motion Control Fabrication

Research Overview

  • Upgrade torque and precision of an existing SCARA arm using cycloidal gear reduction.
  • Develop two cycloidal gearboxes for Joint 1 and Joint 2.
  • Use Teensy 4.1 as the main controller for future real-time motor control.
  • Fabrication and assembly validation of gearbox design (fit, backlash, smooth motion).

Current Progress

Mechanical

  • Designed cycloidal gearbox for 2 joints.
  • Fabricated and assembled one gearbox.
  • Testing alignment, bearing fit, and smooth rotation.

Electronics & Control

  • Selected Teensy 4.1 as the main microcontroller.
  • Planning motor driver + encoder feedback integration.
  • Preparing software structure for motion control and kinematics.

Technical Details

Cycloidal Gearbox (Joint 1 & Joint 2)

  • Purpose: increase torque and reduce backlash for improved accuracy.
  • Designed for compact integration with SCARA joint structure.
  • Focus: durability, manufacturability, and assembly alignment.

Controller: Teensy 4.1

  • High performance MCU suitable for fast control loops.
  • Planned: encoder feedback, trajectory generation, safety limits.
  • Future: communication (USB/Serial/CAN/etc. if needed).

Next Steps

  • Complete fabrication and assembly of the second gearbox.
  • Measure backlash and efficiency (compare with design expectations).
  • Mount gearboxes to SCARA joints and test mechanical motion.
  • Integrate motor drivers + encoders and start closed-loop control tests.
  • Implement basic kinematics (forward/inverse) and motion profiles.
